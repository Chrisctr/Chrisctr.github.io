After Ajay betrayed him in favour of Barshin, Talidor tied Ajay to a chair and punched him over and over until his knuckles broke. In the end, Talidor executed him after Ajay pointed out his similarities to Barshin.

Unsatisfied by vengeance, Talidor formed a mercenary group called the Huntsmen’s Dogs, expanding the organisation until it exceeded 900 members, at which point the current Venator, Shiomi, hired him and his mercenaries to follow him to the Demon Realm to end the Fifth Demon God Rising before it began. Talidor refused, seeing it as a suicide mission, leading to a witch called Medeia inflicting him with a curse which dictates that he’ll “outlive the universe and be forever shaped in the void of space; everlasting, ever-suffering”. To prove it worked, Shiomi stabbed him through the heart. Though he was comatose, he awoke two weeks later without any medical attention. The curse could only be broken if Talidor himself killed a Demon God. Not wanting to live without end, Talidor accepted Shiomi’s mission, though left behind his team.

Once Talidor and Shiomi reached the Heart of Nyruchrodes, they were attacked by a Sovereign-class Spirit Demon, Yuvi. Upon defeating her, Yuvi cast a spell as a last ditch effort to become the next Demon God, the spell being one of teleportation. However, the spell malfunctioned, sending all three of them to the Demon Realm’s void, where they’d emerge at different points in the future. Shiomi was taken a year into the Demon God Rising, Yuvi has yet to emerge, and Talidor was taken to the year 5969 ADR, 969 years into the future. Over the course of the next 81 years, he formed the resurrected Huntsmen’s Dogs, with the only other ‘living’ founding member being Dominic Thorne, a Human-turned-succubus.

In 6040, he accepted a mission to kidnap a 15-year-old leaf mage called Daisy. Upon hearing what the client wanted from her, he decided to cancel the mission and take Daisy back. As Daisy bonded with him during that time, and because her parents were cruel and abusive, she decided to stay. Talidor surprisingly accepted her as his protege. A few weeks ago, the Huntsmen’s Dogs aerial fortress was destroyed. Knowing he was a target to some powerful people, he decided to lay low for a while. That’s when he received another curse, caused by a black letter sent by the First Knight of Nephthys, Adrien Fall.

Smithin’ For Life

To get started, choose a weapon from the sister app, 'Weapons of Alysia'. For all non-gun weapons, choose a material.

Unprotected

  1. 0. Scrap = 0
  2. 1. Iron = +1 weapon modifier, +1 AC modifier
  3. 2. Steel = +2 weapon modifier, +1 AC modifier
  4. 3. Titanium = +3 weapon modifier, +2 AC modifier
  5. 4. Carbon = +4 weapon modifier, +2 AC modifier

protected

  1. 5. Rigamer = +5 weapon modifier, +3 AC modifier
  2. 6. Crystal = +6 weapon modifier, +3 AC modifier
  3. 7. Royal Steel = +7 weapon modifier, +4 AC modifier
  4. 8. Raferrium = +8 weapon modifier, +4 AC modifier

Each grade of material will double the cost and add an additional 0.5lb of weight, but add an additional damage to the modifier. Scrap weapons cannot be custom made, which is why its grade is zero.

Example:

  • A iron knife (1d4 + 1 slash) = 100 mensi
  • A rigamer knife (1d4 + 5 slash) = 1600 mensi

You can also have your weapon replicated with superior materials for the same cost. However, as rigamer is a protected material, you need to possess the material before you can have the weapon made. Unprotected materials will already be possessed by the manufacturer, meaning this is no concern. If the material is protected, you need one ingot for a mod, two for a simple weapon or bow, three for a gun, and four for a martial weapon.

Guns have a base ammunition capacity, and incur an additional cost to go beyond it. This cost isn't multiplied when choosing a material. Generally speaking, the lower the base capacity and the higher the dice it uses means the greater the cost. Burst, fully automatic, and pulse weapons tend to be made to be expanded. Despite the expense, it is worthwhile as it takes a turn to reload.

Example:

  • Cael Theocracy Service Rifle (rifle rounds) (1d12 pierce) 5 round magazine = 3000 mensi
  • Cael Theocracy Service Rifle (rifle rounds) (1d12 pierce) 8 round magazine = 12000 mensi

However, as there is a nation listed with the name, a delivery cost would be added if you are not currently in that nation. Look below for the costs. If you already own the weapon you want to make, then it will go under replication (this being how you upgrade the materials of a weapon), meaning delivery costs won’t be incurred.

Universal mods can be put on any weapon besides mounted weapons, except for Universal’s Extra mods. These tend to be pretty expensive, but aren’t technically part of the weapon’s construction (meaning it’s excluded when accounting for the material multiplier), and can be removed and put onto a weapon of the same type. You will have to pay an additional delivery cost to have these made, given you are in a different nation and the mod and weapons aren’t from the same place.
